Unfolding the Life Stories of Saiō Princess-Priestess Kishi through Network Analysis

The Saiō princess-priestesses who served the deities at the Grand Shrine of Ise or the Kamo Shrines in Kyoto are sacred figures seen as symbolizing imperial authority and divine worship. They possess a unique position in premodern Japanese history and culture at the intersection of court politics, religious practice, and literary history. Please click the button below to review a network visualization made through Cytoscape.
This visualization displays Saiō Princess Kishi’s connections with imperial family members, courtiers, and other court ladies through poems, thus serving as an analytical framework to examine Saiō's roles in broader social context, particularly outside of their divine duties.
