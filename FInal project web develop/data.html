1. Lab Mouse Colony Management

Mouse colony management stands as a fundamental aspect of everyday laboratory operations. Within our virtual lab, we meticulously set up breeder cages, facilitating the natural process where male and female mice are paired for mating. Approximately three weeks post-mating, a new generation of mice, affectionately known as a litter of pups, is born. As these pups mature, significant growth is observed within the first three weeks of their lives. To uphold the standards of animal welfare, it becomes essential to undertake the process of weaning. This critical step involves carefully separating the baby mice from their parents and relocating them into different cages.
