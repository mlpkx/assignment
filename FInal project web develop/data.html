3. Experimental Data Analysis

We use a technique called flow cytometry to pinpoint specific groups of cells that interest us, which is a crucial first step in understanding how these cells function. Think of a flow cytometry plot as a starry sky, where each staris a cell. The position of each star, or cell, in relation to the axes tells us about the presence and amount of certain markers on that cell. By observing how these stars cluster together, we can identify groups of cells based on their unique markers. For example, regulatory T cells, which are essential in shielding us from harmful immune responses, can be identified by their high levels of two markers: CD4 and Foxp3. Imagine being able to spot these important cells in a flow cytometry plot, much like picking out constellations in the night sky.
