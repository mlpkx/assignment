Lesson: Utilizing Python's difflib for Poetry Analysis

Lesson Goals

In this lesson, you will learn how to utilize Python's "difflib" library to compare different versions of poetry texts. You will learn to read, prepare, and process textual data of poems, and generate a visual representation of differences between versions. This skill is valuable for studying poems in various manuscripts with distinct compositions of Kanji and Kana.

Concept Explanation

Waka is a traditional form of Japanese poetry. It's structured in a pattern of 5-7-5-7-7 syllables. This format gives waka its rhythmic quality. For more insight, explore an example of waka at this link and listen to a traditional recitation here.

The writing system (hyōki) in waka involves kanji and kana. "difflib" helps trace the distinct features of each version for further writing system analysis.

Examples

In the following examples, you will read one poem in three different hyōki systems.

Example 1: 花の色は うつりにけりな いたづらに わが身世にふる ながめせしまに

Example 2: 花の色は うつりにけりな いたづらに わが身よにふる ながめせしまに

Example 3: はなのいろは うつりにけりな いたずらに わがみよにふる ながめせしまに
